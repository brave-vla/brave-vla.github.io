About
Why BRAVE?
Vision-Language-Action models show strong progress in controlled labs and curated benchmarks — yet real-world reliability remains limited. What bottlenecks must be addressed for VLA models to perform robustly in realistic environments?

This workshop brings together the computer vision, language, and robotics communities to align on shared problem definitions, highlight common failure modes, and discuss emerging approaches toward building deployable and robust embodied systems. We extend the discussion beyond controlled laboratory settings — to realistic environments — focusing on bottlenecks and emerging solutions across representation learning, data-centric approaches, and system integration.

In applications such as household mobile manipulation, public-space assistance, and warehouse logistics, models must generalize under occlusions, changing viewpoints, novel objects, and long-horizon task execution. Recent studies reveal limitations including sensitivity to appearance and distribution shifts, shortcut learning from spurious visual cues, and weak language grounding.

Workshop Themes
Key Topics
Computer-vision driven topics at the core of reliable VLA systems.
01

Grounding Vision & Language to Action

Connecting linguistic instructions to visual and physical affordances for meaningful action execution.

02

Generalization Across Tasks & Environments

Transferring learned skills across diverse tasks, scenes, objects, and sensing conditions.

03

Scaling Under Limited Real-World Data

Building capable systems when real-world data is scarce, imperfect, or costly to collect.

04

Data Quality

Ensuring broad, well-aligned vision-language coverage across tasks, scenes, and conditions.

05

Robustness & Safety

Reliable performance under realistic sensing variation, occlusions, and distribution shifts.

06

Fair & Comprehensive Evaluation

Evaluation practices that measure generalization, safety, and reliability beyond standard benchmarks.

Call for Papers

Submit Your Work

We invite submissions addressing any of the workshop themes. Submissions follow the ECCV paper template with a double-blind peer-review process and workshop-specific page limits. Accepted papers will be included in the official proceedings. One selected paper will receive a Workshop Outstanding Paper Award and an oral presentation slot.

We expect approximately 20–30 submissions with a ~50% acceptance rate. Accepted papers will be presented during the dedicated poster session.
